# Investor Pitch Deck Outline — MindSports Academy

## Slide 1 — Title
MindSports Academy™ — The Global Platform for Strategic Board Games.
Tagline: "Duolingo for learning. Chess.com for competition. Coursera for mastery. One academy for every strategic mind."

## Slide 2 — The Problem
- Chess.com/Lichess: world-class play, weak structured pedagogy.
- Duolingo-style apps: great learning UX, can't host real competitive depth.
- Traditional/cultural strategy games (Oware, Xiangqi, Shogi) have no modern AI-coached home.
- No platform unifies learning + competition + certification + community across more than one strategic game.

## Slide 3 — The Insight
Players don't just want to play a game well — they want to *become* strategic thinkers. That skill transfers across games. No one has built the platform around that insight.

## Slide 4 — The Product
Demo screenshots/flow: Learn → Practice → Analyze → Test → Certify → Advance, shown on the chess module (Phase 1 product), with the multi-game shell visible (Go/Oware/Shogi "coming soon" tiles) to signal the roadmap is real, not aspirational.

## Slide 5 — Why Now
- AI coaching (LLMs + voice synthesis) makes 1:1-quality coaching economically deliverable at scale for the first time.
- Mobile-first, PWA-capable infra makes global distribution (including emerging markets) low-cost.
- Cultural-games-as-edtech is an open category — no incumbent has claimed it.

## Slide 6 — Market Size
- Chess: ~600M+ players globally (Chess.com/FIDE estimates), large existing paid-learning spend (Chess.com, courses, coaches).
- Go/Shogi/Xiangqi: hundreds of millions of players across Asia, currently underserved by modern coaching platforms.
- Oware/Mancala family: large but almost entirely unaddressed by any venture-backed platform — open field.
- TAM framing: global strategic-games learning + edtech + competitive gaming overlap, not just "chess apps."

## Slide 7 — Business Model
Subscription (core) + Coach Marketplace take rate + Institutional licensing + Certification fees. Single account/subscription value compounds as each new game ships (see [Monetization Strategy](08-monetization-strategy.md) §7).

## Slide 8 — Go-to-Market
- Phase 1: Chess Academy direct-to-consumer + school pilots.
- Coach-led supply-side seeding for marketplace cold start.
- Phase 3 African market entry via Oware — culturally distinct acquisition channel with no direct competitor.
- Phase 4 Asian market entry via Go/Shogi/Xiangqi.

## Slide 9 — Competitive Landscape
Matrix: Chess.com / Lichess / Duolingo / Coursera / OGS (Go) vs. MindSports Academy — showing none combine AI coaching + certification + schools + coach marketplace + multi-game breadth (see [Multi-Game Expansion Framework](12-multi-game-expansion-framework.md) §1 framing).

## Slide 10 — Moat
1. Academy Layer architecture — every new game compounds the platform instead of fragmenting it.
2. Strategic Intelligence Rating — a cross-game credential no competitor can replicate without the same architecture.
3. First-mover on culturally underserved games (Oware) and AI-coached pedagogy depth.
4. Certification + school + marketplace network effects.

## Slide 11 — Traction / Milestones (update live with real metrics post-launch)
- Placeholder structure: signups, D7/D30 retention, certification completion rate, coach marketplace GMV, NPS.

## Slide 12 — Team
Founder/operator backgrounds, advisors (chess titled players, edtech operators, relevant cultural-games community leaders).

## Slide 13 — Roadmap
Condensed version of [24-Month Roadmap](10-24-month-roadmap.md): Chess MVP → Checkers → Oware → Go/Shogi/Xiangqi → Scale.

## Slide 14 — Financials / Ask
- Use of funds: engineering (core platform + AI infra), content (curriculum/certification authoring), GTM (school pilots, coach seeding), Phase 3/4 game expansion.
- Funding ask sized to reach Phase 4 (Month 22) milestones with runway buffer.

## Slide 15 — Vision (Close)
```
MindSports Academy → Global MindSports Federation → International Certifications
   → School Partnerships → University Scholarships → Professional Leagues
```
"We're not building a chess app. We're building the academy every strategic mind grows up in."
